Locket

Never again wonder what you'd do if you had the time.

Summary

Locket is a web app that helps us set personal goals, track progress, and celebrate our everyday achievements. This self-contained system is free of the distractions, unsolicited opinions, and hasty judgments of the wider world online. It doesn’t do more than it needs to, and shares nothing before we’re ready. It is a safe space where we can, over time, turn an everyday idea into something extraordinary.

Problem

In 2019, we have a mind-boggling array of tools for planning our daily lives, staying on task, and tracking our progress with personal goals. We can sync our Google Calendar with iCal, copy it all to our Trello account - and have all three platforms reminding us to do what we must do in life. Why is it that we still fall behind, forget important things, and generally fail to meet our expectations for ourselves? Is this the sort of problem that can be solved with an app? It doesn’t hurt to try.

I came up with the idea for this product while battling my own difficulty completing projects. I have a hundred ideas for creative and self-improvement projects each year, but only a fraction of these are seen through to fruition. Surely other creative people struggle with the same issue. What is the root of the problem, and can it be adjusted for? I carried the entire project from the research phase through visual design, prototyping and testing.

Solution

When asked about the biggest barriers to accomplishing personal goals, people routinely cite the overwhelming, round-the-clock demands of work. Everyone needs a way to completely detach from their workday in order to focus on personal projects. Making personal work public too early in the creative process, as with social sharing, appears to have a detrimental effect as well. It can be easy to internalize the criticisms of others, allow self-doubt to build, and abandon projects early and often. Locket solves these problems by operating independently of all other calendars, and by the absence of social sharing functionality. When the app is in use, the user has no choice but to focus on themselves. All other platforms are locked, and associated notifications are delayed during project hours.

User Research

Almost everyone who participated in my survey wishes they had more time for personal creative projects. Respondents are working on things like:

  • UX Design Projects
  • Case Studies
  • Fine Art
  • Visual Design Work
  • Illustrations
  • Photography.

When asked about the biggest obstacle they face, the vast majority of those surveyed cited their job. To a lesser degree, a lack of funds, general lack of free time and family obligations were also barriers to getting things done.

Interestingly, many respondents also cited a lack of motivation and frustration with their own skill level as roadblocks to achieving their goals. Very few considered social media to be the root of the problem. However, more than 90% of respondents felt they could benefit from an app that delayed notifications – or locked their phone completely (aside from emergency calls) – during their scheduled project hours.

Additionally, the vast majority of respondents did not care to have their achievements featured in a blog post, or posted on an online forum. They also would rather not waste time reading related articles. This suggests that most people would prefer to work on creative projects anonymously and independently – safe from unwanted criticism and other outside influences.

Competitive Analysis

Google is ubiquitous. It’s where everyone goes to do just about anything, and all products are fully integrated into a collection of free tools with widespread appeal. Google provides free services in exchange for user data. The more free services they provide, the more information can be gathered on the habits and preferences of individual users. This information can then be used to funnel targeted ads or other content to users. The end result is a personalized experience across platforms such as YouTube, Gmail and Google Maps. Some users may find the endless data collection invasive, or the targeted content distracting - especially when trying to make time for personal goals.

Apple products are also everywhere you look, even though they are angled slightly toward the luxury market when compared to most Android or Google products. Calendar, Reminders and Notes come preloaded with Apple devices, and are fully integrated with other applications by Apple. Again, these are essentially a collection of free tools with widespread appeal. Everything you’ve ever scheduled, noted, or listed is stored somewhere for safe-keeping. If you are interested in extra features, such as sketching, color coding, importing images etc., they already exist and only await your discovery.

Trello is a project management platform, owned by Atlassian. It is really aimed at team projects. However, there is an interesting option available to create personal project tracking cards on boards, called “Life Goals.” These boards allow you to pick a custom background image from Unsplash, sort of like a hero image for your projects. These boards are separate from your shared, professional boards, and can be marked private. Trello is taking the functionality of the Apple and Google products and allowing users to arrange things their own way, on art boards that are visually appealing and fun.

This in itself may be enough to make a user feel like they are in a non-work, customized creative space Just the simple act of setting up the board may spark other creative ideas, help a user understand how they feel about their projects, or allow them to visualize the end result of their hard work. If a product can carve out a space away from anything work-related, which also minimizes or eliminates distractions, it might be useful. The lower it is in cost, the more likely users will adapt to it. A combination of functionality, privacy and style might encourage users to pay $5 per month. The UI should feel experimental and fun, and be the kind of space that encourages creativity.

Personas

Since over 80% of those surveyed who expressed an interest in a product like this were under 35 and female, I decided to focus on this specific audience when crafting my personas.

LISA

  • Age: 23
  • Occupation: Teacher
  • Status: Single
  • Location: Miami

GOALS

Study for One Hour Per Day | Increase to Three Hours Per Day in Summer | Save $200 per week | Go to the gym three times per week | At 100 hours of study, get formal training in coding – or an internship.

NEEDS

Schedule time for herself after work and on weekends | Reduce distractions during study time | Keep her goals private from her coworkers and students | Build confidence by tracking progress and seeing results.

UNDERSTANDING

Lisa uses a classroom cloud for work. She spends over ten hours a day online, and has been using devices since early childhood. Alhough she wants a clear divide between her personal and working lives online, she also wants seamless integration across devices.

MOTIVATIONS

Lisa moved to Miami after signing on with Teach for America. Although she believes that quality education for all is critical to our collective well-being, she often feels drained and even upset at the end of the day. Lisa sometimes feels powerless to make a difference in her community, mostly due to school policies that restrict what and how she can teach in her own classroom. She is considering learning to code in her free time, and may change careers if it goes well. It can be hard to set aside time since she often takes her work home with her.

Conclusion

I hope the latest version of the Locket productivity app is a peaceful, calming, private and fun space. I hope it empowers young women to develop their ideas before sharing them with the world. Although products with charm and style can evoke positive feelings and help spark creativity, building a UI that is decorative will prove to be as distracting as any other online content. When navigating the complex web of emotions that come up while pursuing goals, users will benefit most from a platform that is calming and functional. In the end, Locket will have solved a problem if it steps back and allows the work of the users to shine through.
